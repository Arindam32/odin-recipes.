Greek-Style Shrimp Pasta with Kale

  • 1 (28-ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 6 garlic cloves,thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 2 teaspoons chopped oregano
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 1/2 tsp. kosher salt, plus more
  • 8 ounces shell pasta
  • 1 large bunch of curly kale, stems removed, torn intobute-sized pieces( about 10 cups)
  • 1 1/2 pounds large shrips,peeled, deveined
  • 2 1/2 ounces crumbled feta( about 1/2 cups)
  1. Pour tamatoes into a fine-mesh sieve set over a medium bowl. Shake to release as much juice as possible, then let tomatoesdrain in sieve, collenting juices in bowl, until dready to use.
  2. Heat 2 TBSP. oil in a large Dutch oven or other large heavy pot over high. Add drained tomatoes and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, untill they begun to caramelize, about 12 mintues. Add wine and cook, scraping up browned bits, until reduced be half, about 1 minute. Add collected tomato juices, Oregano, red pepper, and 1 1/2 tsp. salt and bring to a simmer. Cook, stirring occasionally, untill slightly thickened about 5 mintues.
  3. Meanwhile, cook,pasta in a large pot of boiling salted waterm stirring occasionally, until very al dente, 3 minutes less than package directions, Drain, reserving 1 cup pasta cooking liquid.
  4. Working in batches, add kale to tomato sauce, stirring after each addition, until all kale is wilted. Usining a spider or slotted sppon, transfer pasta to pot with sauce. Add shrimp, cover and cook, stirring occasionallyand adding pasta cooking liquid if needed to loosen sauce, until pasta is al dente, shrimp are cooked throughm and everything is nicely coated in sauce, about 3 mintues
  5. Divide pasta amoung bowls. Top with feta and drizzle with oil
